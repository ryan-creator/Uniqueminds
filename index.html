About Me

My interest in learning differences started in 2007 after two of my four boys were assessed with dyslexia. The lack of information and support available lead me to work with other local parents to start a support group for parents of children with learning differences. Our bottom line was to support the parents of any child not able to reach their full potential with their learning even though they were clearly intelligent and capable children.

Unique Minds was established to help children, parents, schools and adults understand these learning differences and support them to reach their full potential.

In this journey I found I was dyslexic. Even though I learnt to read and write I still find many aspects of my life were impacted by my dyslexia. Understanding how my brain works has helped me find acceptance and to embrace my way of thinking.

My work in the field of learning differences continues as owner of Unique Minds, a facilitator for the children’s workshop Growing Stars, as well as, the Learning Differences Advisor for Learning Differences Aotearoa Trust who run the parent support group Dyslexia Support Southland and the Adult Dyslexia Support Group.
